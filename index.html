I finally completed my drone!

After long nights, nearly bursting into tears and broken bits and bobs I finally had my drone up and running by the 12th of August! I felt so proud. This had taken a very long time, I started about two years ago and had given up along the way due to difficulty and common exams at school. Before heading to EMF camp I thought, we are in the middle of a field, a great flying area, and there must be people that can help to fix the drone. Boy was I right. Although I didn't completely fix it I had learn't so much about what to do when building a drone, tips and tricks and a crazy amount of schematics. I ordered the pieces I needed so when I got home they were ready to be used on the drone. Within the next week I had completed and flown a drown enough times to say "I have successfully built my own drone!". I was so happy when completing and still can't understand how I built a flying robot all from scratch!

Lesson 1: Don't fly close to apples!

I was flying my drone in our garden, farely fast, and I hit the basketball post, making the drone take a full stop and crash upside down. There was no damage so I took off once again but didn't realise one of the propellers was resting on an apple. The apple got caught onto the propeller and the speed of the motor sliced the apple. Suprisingly the motor stopped and didn't completely slice the apple, I would of thought the apple had no chance for taking on a powerful motor.

Bought an old Arcade Machine

I have always wanted to have an arcade machine in my room, although I have never been into games. It is quite bizzare, but you'll slowly understand. About a year ago, using the RaspberryPi to set up RetroPie, a way to run emulators and their respectable roms, I had set it up with a monitor, keyboard, mouse and a WIFI dongle. I had installed RetroPie and everything was running perfectly, I even had a gaming table just to store the RetroPie and the SNES controllers, I configured. I became addicted to playing on the Pi and my parents weren't happy that I was prioritising the games over my school work, neither was I. So I decided to pack it away and only take it out if I wanted to play in the school holidays or when friends visited. I kept thinking of a way I could keep the games out and always accessible, slowly I thought of buying an arcade machine that I would keep in my room, being the biggest in the house and I could play whenever I desired. After doing loads of research, the arcade machines ranged from £500 - £4000 ,obviously this was crazy for an arcade machine and kept on thinking. After coming across an old, broken arcade machine I came up with the idea of using the frame to build my own arcade cabinet using the RetroPie as the brain of the project. I came upon this idea, the same inspiration for building my drone. It is always cheaper to build something and put in the effort than be lazy and buy it.

Emptying the whole machine

After opening the machine, I realised that it was too disgusting to use, being made on the 27/9/1999, and being left in someone's garage for a few years, untouched. I decided to completely strip the machine so I would be left with just the cabinet itself, my brother, Manoj spent the whole day with me breaking and disposing the internal computer systems. After doing a lot research I realised that the front panel was curved and needed to be fitted with a CRT monitor (19"). Unfortunately, these were very expensive and even using sites like Gumtree did not provide any realiable results. This was my next problem that needed to be solved.

Painted the machine!

The machine didn't have a very appealing look and even if I were to add my own electronics to make it look like new, a paint job was necessary. After going to B and Q to pick up some supplies, I started painting the machine. Awesome thing was, the arcade machine was flat on it's back ontop of two old desktop tower computers for support. Old tech which was not needed but still kept for times of service.
