FabLab London

I attended an event at the FabLab London, which was one of the key events that changed my view on technological innovation. (for the better) This event opened my eyes to the technology that could help less fortunate, or those from LED's, less-economically developed countries. Technology is not accessible to them as it is for us so being part of a team whose main thesis was to be able to provide LED's or countries that had just experienced a natural disaster with the same Wi-Fi bandwith that we all take for granted today. Once I saw I was able to help the less fortunate with something I use all the time and this would be part of a better community, I knew I wanted to be a part of the technology industry.

Techcrunch Disrupt EU
2014
This hackathon was by far the best because of all the huge companies that had sponsored it, like Paypal, (include here the other companies....) and Techcrunch themselves, of course. They had held the event at a historical venue, where the James Bond Skyfall prison scene was filmed, with the underground tunnels. Having access to a huge amount of kit, which myself and my brother brought to the event gave us a headstart in coming up with projects to do for the two-event day. However, I spent a lot of time understanding and using an Arduino from the original handbook whilst everyone else at the event worked on their projects. I made sure that by the end of the event I was confident in troubleshooting and the basics of using an Arduino. I was also introduced to the Raspberry Pi which allowed me to add UI's to any project I wanted. It had felt like I was in hardware-heaven.
